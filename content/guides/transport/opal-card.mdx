An Opal card is a reusable smartcard that covers almost every form of public
transport in Sydney: trains, buses, ferries, light rail, and the Metro. You
tap on when you board and tap off when you leave — fares are calculated
automatically, and the more you travel in a week, the cheaper each trip gets.

## Getting a card

You can pick up a physical Opal card for free from any staffed station,
most newsagents, and convenience stores displaying the Opal sign. No ID or
paperwork is required to get a card — you only need one when you register it
online, which is optional but recommended in case it's ever lost or stolen.

If you'd rather skip the physical card entirely, you can add an Opal digital
card to Apple Pay or Google Pay directly from your phone and start tapping on
straight away.

<Callout>
  Heads up: contactless bank cards and linked phone wallets also work
  directly on Opal readers, without needing an Opal card at all — useful if
  you're only in Sydney for a short trip.
</Callout>

## Loading money on

Top up a physical card with cash at station machines, newsagents, or online
once registered. Digital cards top up automatically from the linked card or
can be topped up manually the same way.

## Do this now

Grab a physical Opal card from any staffed station or newsagent today, or add
an Opal digital card to your phone's wallet — either way, you'll be ready to
tap on for your first trip within minutes.
